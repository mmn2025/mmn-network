About Morocco

Morocco (Arabic: المغرب, al-Maḡrib; Berber: ⵍⵎⵖⵔⵉⴱ, Lmeɣrib) is a country located in the northwestern corner of Africa. With a population of over 37 million, it is known for its rich history, diverse culture, and strategic location at the intersection of Africa, Europe, and the Arab world.

Administrative Regions

Morocco is a constitutional monarchy with an elected parliament. The King of Morocco holds significant executive and religious powers as both the political leader and the "Commander of the Faithful." The country's economy is diverse, including agriculture, tourism, aerospace, phosphate mining, textiles, and telecommunications.

Moroccan Mathematics and Science

Morocco has a rich mathematical and scientific tradition dating back to the medieval period, when scholars in cities like Fez, Marrakech, and Tetouan made significant contributions to mathematics, astronomy, and medicine. Today, Morocco continues to develop its scientific infrastructure and mathematical education system.

11th-12th Centuries

Al-Murrakushi

Moroccan mathematicians and astronomers made significant contributions to spherical trigonometry and astronomical tables.

13th-14th Centuries

Ibn al-Banna

Born in Marrakech, he wrote important works on algebra, arithmetic, and astronomy, including "Talkhis amal al-hisab" (Summary of Arithmetical Operations).

Major Universities and Research Centers

Morocco has a well-established network of universities and research centers with strong mathematics departments, contributing to the advancement of mathematical sciences in North Africa and beyond.

Mohammed V University in Rabat

One of Morocco's oldest and most prestigious universities, with strong departments in pure and applied mathematics, including algebra, analysis, and probability theory.

Cultural and Natural Highlights

Beyond its academic achievements, Morocco offers a rich tapestry of cultural and natural wonders that reflect its unique position at the crossroads of different civilizations and landscapes.

Historic Medinas

Morocco's ancient walled cities, such as Fez, Marrakech, Tetouan, and Essaouira, are UNESCO World Heritage sites known for their intricate architecture, vibrant souks, and rich history.

Atlas Mountains

The majestic mountain range running through Morocco offers breathtaking landscapes, traditional Berber villages, and Toubkal, North Africa's highest peak at 4,167 meters.

Sahara Desert

The world's largest hot desert extends into southern Morocco, offering stunning dune landscapes at places like Merzouga and Zagora, and opportunities for astronomical observation.

Culinary Traditions

Renowned for its flavorful dishes like tagine, couscous, and pastilla, Moroccan cuisine reflects the country's diverse cultural influences and rich agricultural heritage.
